Historical Market Views: Replaying Past
Market Events for Enhanced Risk Insight
Understanding how a firm's investments react to market movements that replicate past critical stress events is one of the most intuitive and powerful forms of risk analysis. Regulators, practitioners, executives and investors all appreciate the need to protect a portfolio against 'another crash' or 'another market correction.' The value of a comprehensive full evaluation risk model is that this is possible while maintaining transparency and tractibility.
Sophisticated Scenario Generation: Using Copulas to Model Risk Factor Dependence
Risk practitioners have long known that markets don’t move (or co-move) according to a normal distribution. Recent volatility and instability on global financial markets gives even more evidence to the notion that asset and portfolio returns exhibit significant kurtosis and skewness. To address this, Algo Risk Service includes functional components to handle a variety of Copula-based scenario generation techniques including the Gaussian Copula, Student t Copula and IT Copula.
Comparitive Risk Analysis: Using the Multi-Context Reporting Feature
For risk practitioners, understanding the level of portfolio risk is as important as understanding how that risk measure is moving over time. Algo Risk Service now offers a multi-context reporting feature which facilitates the display and analysis of period over period risk results directly in the reporting application.
Decomposing Value-at-Risk (VaR): Contributing to Portfolio Risk Analytics
Underlying a portfolio level risk calculation are the contributions made by each security, market segment and asset class. In order to understand how these results roll up to an aggregated figure, one can examine the profit and loss figures given by a specific VaR scenario. These results can be particularly volatile from simulation to simulation. The Algo Risk Application reporting interface allows users to apply scenario weighting schemes to mitigate the dependency of these figures on one specific scenario outcome.

Transparency and granularity is essential to modeling commodity based financial products. The risk on strategies that include bets on supply and demand through time (‘calendar spreads’) or the economic relationship between underlying products (‘intercommodity spreads’) can be improperly measured if models fail to capture all market information that is relevant. Algo Risk Service uses a commodity forward model whereby forward curves are tracked explicitly and returns modeled via a ‘rolling nearby’ process giving a more accurate and transparent risk result.
