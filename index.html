Instrument Look-Through: Enhancing Model Auditability
Increased scrutiny by investors, auditors and regulators on the quality of portfolio valuations and risk calculations means that model and data transparency is a key requirement of risk systems. The new Instrument Look-Through feature in IBM Algo Risk Service on Cloud helps address these challenges by exposing valuations, parameters and methodologies to the appropriately privileged end user. The Stochastic Process Module: Valuing Complex Derivatives Accurately
Complex path-dependent options can present challenges in valuation and even greater difficulties in risk assessment. The stochastic process module (SPM) used by IBM Algo Risk Service on Cloud allows users to value and calculate risk measures for complex products that require Monte Carlo based pricing, particularly useful when closed form solutions are impossible to derive.
The Use of User Defined Grids in the Simulation of Complex Financial Instruments
Valuation of derivatives with complex cash flows and exotic features can be computationally demanding. Standard techniques such as Monte Carlo valuation can be used, but to obtain risk analytics for a portfolio of these instruments, the stochastic-on-stochastic processing effort required can be overwhelming. The use of low dimensional grids allows for an alternative simulation approach for resource-intensive instruments.
Historical scenarios: Model Independent Risk Analysis
Historical Scenario analysis is one of the most popular and accessible forms of statistical risk measurement. The choice of parameters such as the returns period, sampling method and volatility weighting each imply different assumptions about the past and may lead to different results. Understanding the impact of these choices is important in order to interpret the results accurately. IBM Algo Risk Service on Cloud allows a flexible framework through which to develop and apply historical scenarios across a broad risk factor set.
Change Management Policy in IBM Algo Risk Service on Cloud: Ensuring Quality and Productivity
The assumptions and methods behind market risk analysis can vary broadly amongst market segments, jurisdictions and companies. Consider also that the risk process needs to evolve over time and that practitioners may move at different speeds at using new or unfamiliar techniques. Change can be fraught with many forms of operational risks. To address this risk, IBM Algo Risk Service on Cloud has a documented change management policy that is followed for all client production environmental deployments.
